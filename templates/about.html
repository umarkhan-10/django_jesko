⚙️Engine & Powertrain

       1: 15.0 L twin-turbo V8, flat-plane 180° crankshaft, dry sump lubrication, with 2.2 bar boost on E85 and 1.7 bar regular.


       2: Engine weight is just ~189 kg, featuring innovative air injection to spool turbos for instant throttle response, and individual
          cylinder pressure sensors for optimized tuning.


       3: Output: ~1,280 hp on gasoline, rising to ~1,578–1,600 hp on E85 biofuel. Torque: ~1,000 Nm between 2,700–6,170 rpm, peaking at
          1,500 Nm at 5,100 rpm.


       4: Paired with Koenigsegg’s revolutionary 9-speed Light Speed Transmission (LST)—a multi-clutch gearbox capable of gear skips with
          near-instant shifts and weighing just ~90 kg.

📐 Dimensions & Weight

       1: Wheelbase: 2,700 mm | Length: ~4,610 mm | Width: ~2,030 mm | Height: ~1,210 mm.


       2: Dry weight ranges from 1,290–1,320 kg, depending on variant; curb weight approx 1,390–1,420 kg.

🌪 Performance Highlights & Records

       1: Koenigsegg claims both variants capable of exceeding 300 mph (480 km/h+), with target simulations for Absolut
          approaching 531 km/h (~330 mph).


       2: Official world record: 0–400 km/h + back to 0 in 27.83 seconds, set by Markus Lundh in a Jesko Absolut at Örebro Airport,
          Sweden (June 26, 2024).


       3: 0–400 km/h in 18.82 s, 0–250 mph in 19.20 s, and full 400 to 0 in 9.01 s—all records for production cars.

💶 Price & Exclusivity

       1: Initial list price around US $3 million (approx. €2.8 million or ₹70+ crore in India) depending on customization and
          regional market.


       2: Only 125 units, fully sold out; some special editions like “Hydra” and “Odin” also exist.

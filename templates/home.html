    The Koenigsegg Jesko is a cutting-edge Swedish hypercar built for extreme performance, both on the
    track and the road. Named after Jesko von Koenigsegg, the father of company founder Christian von Koenigsegg,
    the Jesko was unveiled at the 2019 Geneva Motor Show as the successor to the Agera RS.

    Under the hood, the Jesko boasts a 5.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine capable of producing up to
    1600 horsepoweron E85 fuel and 1280 hp on standard gasoline. One of its standout features is the
    Light Speed Transmission (LST) — a 9-speed multi-clutch gearbox that allows for nearly instant gear
    changes in any order, without the lag of traditional dual-clutch systems. The Jesko is available in two
    versions: the Jesko Absolut, built for maximum top speed, and the **Jesko Attack**, optimized for downforce
    and track performance.

    With advanced aerodynamics, carbon fiber construction, active suspension, and industry-leading engineering,
    the Jesko is designed to potentially exceed speeds of 480 km/h (300 mph), making it one of the fastest
    production cars in the world. The cabin includes modern infotainment, climate control, and lightweight
    luxury materials, offering comfort alongside brutal performance. Production is extremely limited, and prices
    start at over $3 million USD. The Jesko isn’t just a car — it’s a showcase of Koenigsegg’s engineering
    genius and obsession with perfection.
